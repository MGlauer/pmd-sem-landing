# Developing Interoperable Application Ontologies

By following established guidelines and using common design patterns, developers can create ontologies that are consistent, reusable, and capable of working seamlessly in collaborative and cross-disciplinary environments.

To support ontology developers in this process, we provide two comprehensive resources:

- **General Guide for Collaborative Ontology Development**  
  A step-by-step guide to best practices in collaborative ontology design, ensuring consistency and interoperability across domains. [Access the guide](https://scientific-ontology-network.github.io/)

- **Materials Science & PMDco Specific Guide**  
  Tailored guidance for ontology development within the context of materials science and PMDco, including recommended workflows and alignment principles. [Access the guide](https://materialdigital.github.io/core-ontology/docs/PMDco%20User%20Guide/#how-to-develop-your-application-ontologies-using-pmdco-and-oboodk-best-practices)

By following these resources, developers can create application ontologies that are not only robust and consistent but also integrable with other ontologies in the field.
